About Us

Greetings in St. La Salle! We are Computer Engineering students from De La Salle University working on an innovative research project. Our study focuses on developing a Vision-Based Seat Occupancy Detection and Visualization System for point-to-point (P2P) buses in the Philippines.

Why This Project Matters

Public transportation plays a huge role in daily life, but passengers often face challenges like overcrowding and inefficient scheduling. Unlike other buses, P2P buses donâ€™t have seat reservations, making it hard to predict available seating. Our system aims to solve this by providing near real-time seat occupancy updates to help both passengers and bus operators.

What Our System Can Do

  • For Commuters: Before getting on a bus, passengers can check real-time seat availability, allowing them to plan a more comfortable and hassle-free commute.
  • For Bus Companies: Operators can use this data to optimize bus schedules, improve fleet management, and reduce congestion in high-traffic areas.
  • For Computer Engineers: Our research provides a foundation for exploring new technologies, including computer vision, machine learning, and artificial intelligence, while addressing real-world transportation challenges.
  • For Government Agencies: Data from our system can help agencies like MMDA and DPWH in policy-making, traffic planning, and enforcing safety regulations to ensure smoother and more efficient public transport.

Our Goal

By introducing this system, we hope to enhance passenger experience, improve bus operations, and support data-driven decision-making in transportation. With real-time seat tracking, we believe we can make commuting more convenient, efficient, and future-ready.
